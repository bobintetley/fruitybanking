Fruity Banking!

Fruity Banking is a web-based personal finance system based on double-entry accounting methods.

You create accounts, which can represent "real" accounts (such as credit cards, bank accounts, etc.) and "nominal" accounts (expense and income accounts).

If you are unfamiliar with double entry accounting, the basic idea is that money cannot simply appear or disappear, every single transaction has two sides - a source and destination account. You take money from one and put it into the other.

Example

Say, I have a bank account called "Current" and a nominal expense account called "House Expenses". I could go to the shops and spend 48 pounds on shopping against my debit card. I log this by taking the money I spent from the "Current" account (decrementing the amount of money I have in there) and putting it into the "House Expenses" account. We do this by logging a withdrawal against the current account with the house expenses as the destination on the transaction screen for the current account.

Because every transaction has two sides, after I log that transaction, I could view the house expenses account and the transaction would appear as a deposit in there.

By sourcing money from nominal accounts in this way, you can track how much you've spent on different items!

Reconciliation

Whilst viewing any account, if you have a statement to show real transactions, you can follow the reconciliation link to mark the transaction as reconciled against the statement.
